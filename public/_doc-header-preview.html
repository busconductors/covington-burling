Design Decisions

Element Before After Why
Header variants 4 confusing letter-named variants (A/B/C/D) 1 professional header Users shouldn't need to pick from cryptic variants
Margins 50pt (pdf-lib) vs 72pt (pdfmake) — inconsistent 72pt all around (1 inch) — legal standard Consistent across all documents; 1" is the legal-document standard
Body font Times (pdfmake) vs Cormorant Garamond (pdf-lib) — mismatch Cormorant Garamond everywhere Brand font, embedded in all documents
Body size 10pt / 11pt inconsistent 11.5pt body, 11pt clauses Readable but not oversized; professional legal density
Line height 1.55 (tight) 1.7–1.75 More breathing room; easier to read dense legal text
Clause gap 8pt (way too tight) 18pt between clauses Clear visual separation between clauses
Clause indent 0pt (pdfmake) vs 24pt (pdf-lib) 28pt body indent Legal convention; clarifies clause boundaries
Contact line Serif font (pdfmake Times) Montserrat sans-serif Matches brand identity; small text is more legible in sans
Title style 14pt bold, no underline (pdfmake) / 13pt with gold rule (pdf-lib) 15pt uppercase, gold rule underline Polished, distinguished, on-brand
Signature area Tight, cramped labels 38pt per field row, clear hierarchy Professional execution-block conventions
Footer Inconsistent margins; 6.5pt text 7.5pt uppercase, anchored to bottom margin Clean, professional, legible at small sizes
